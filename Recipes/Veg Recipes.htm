Baked eggs

Time Needed: 40 minutes
Ingredients
fresh baby spinach (2 cups or 1 very large handful per person)
eggs - 2 large eggs per person
heavy whipping cream (1 Tablespoon per person)
 salt and pepper
bacon bits - 1 teaspoon per person
finely grated parmesan cheese - 1 Tablespoon per person

Directions: Preheat oven to 350. Boil water, then add some salt to water and cook the spinach for 1 minute. Drain spinach well and get as much of the water out of it as possible. Rub butter inside 10-12 ounce ramekins (this is a small baking dish with straight sides ... anything small that will go in the oven and hold the spinach and eggs should work fine). Each person will have their own ramekin. Put spinach in the ramekins, making a little indention in the middle of the spinach so the eggs will nestle into the spinach. Break two eggs into each dish on top of the spinach. Add a pinch of salt and a grind of pepper to each dish, then add the bacon bits and whipping cream. Bake in oven for 14 minutes. Add the parmesan cheese and return to oven for 2 minutes. If you don't like your egg yolks runny, add about 2-3 minutes to the cooking time. Serve hot.

Broccoli and most other cooked vegetables

Time Needed: 5 - 20 minutes (Spinach - 2 minutes; Broccoli - 5 minutes; Pole beans - 20 minutes)
Ingredients
fresh vegetables
salt (about a teaspoon)

Directions: Rinse off the vegetables. Prepare the vegetables for cooking. For broccoli, cut into spears. For spinach, remove any large stems. For pole beans, string and snap into pieces. Put a pot of water on to boil. The pot should be large enough to hold the vegetables with room to spare. Once the water is boiling, add the salt to the water. Then add the broccoli or whatever vegetable you want to cook. Do not put the top back on the pot. Cook for just a few minutes until you can stick a fork in the vegetable without a lot of resistance (this is called fork tender). As soon as this occurs, drain the vegetables and serve immediately. If you aren't going to eat them all at this meal, rinse what you don't plan to eat with cold water. This will keep it from continuing to cook from the heat.

Brussels Sprouts with Browned Butter and Hazelnuts

Time Needed: 25-30 minutes
Ingredients
3 Tablespoons unsalted butter
1 pound Brussesl sprouts, trimmed and quartered
1/4 cup chopped hazelnuts
3 Tablespoons water

Directions: Position rack in bottom third of oven and preheat to 450ºF. Place butter on a rimmed baking pan that is large enough for the Brussels sprouts to roast in a single layer. Roast until the butter is melted, browned and fragrant, 4-6 minutes.

Remove the baking sheet from the oven. Toss Brussels sprouts and hazelnuts with the browned butter and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Return to the oven and roast for 7 minutes. Caesar Salad (vegetarian)

Time Needed: 20-30 minutes - most can be done ahead
Ingredients
18 to 24 crisp, narrow leaves from the hearts of 2 heads of romaine lettuce, or a package of romaine hearts (about 1 pound)
1 cup plain toasted croutons
1 large clove garlic, peeled
1/4 cup or more excellent olive oil
salt
 1 large egg
freshly ground black pepper
1 whole lemon, halved and seeded
Worcestershire sauce
2 tablespoons freshly grated parmesan cheese, imported Parmigiano-Reggiano only

Directions:

Preparing the salad components: You will probably need 2 large heads of romaine for 3 people — or use a commercially prepared package of "romaine hearts," if they appear fresh and fine. From a large head remove the outside leaves until you get down to the cone where the leaves are 4 to 7 inches in length — you'll want 6 to 8 of these leaves per serving. Separate the leaves and wash them carefully to keep them whole, roll them loosely in clean towels, and keep refrigerated until serving time. (Save the remains for other salads — fortunately, romaine keeps reasonably well under refrigeration.

To flavor the croutons, crush the garlic clove with the flat of a chef's knife, sprinkle on 1/4 teaspoon of salt, and mince well. Pour about a tablespoon of olive oil on the garlic and mash again with the knife, rubbing and pressing to make a soft purée.

Scrape the purée into the frying pan, add another tablespoon of oil, and warm over low-medium heat. Add the croutons and toss for a minute or two to infuse them with the garlic oil, then remove from the heat. (For a milder garlic flavor, you can strain the purée though a small sieve into a pan before adding the extra croutons. Discard the bits of garlic.)

To coddle the egg, bring a small saucepan of water to a simmer. Pierce the large end of the egg with a pushpin to prevent cracking, then simmer for exactly 1 minute.

Mixing and serving the Caesar: Dress the salad just before serving. Have ready all the dressing ingredients and a salad fork and spoon for tossing.

Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the romaine leaves and toss to coat, lifting the leaves from the bottom and turning them towards you, so they tumble over like a wave. Sprinkle them with a generous pinch of salt and several grinds of pepper, toss once or twice, then add the lemon juice and several drops of the Worcestershire, and toss again. Taste for seasoning, and add more, if needed.

Crack the egg and drop it right on the romaine leaves, then toss to break it up and coat the leaves. Sprinkle on the cheese, toss briefly, then add the croutons (and the garlicky bits in the pan, if you wish) and toss for the last time, just to mix them into the salad.

Arrange 6 or more leaves in a single layer on individual plates, scatter the croutons all around, and serve. Serves 2-3. (Recipe reprinted from Julia Child and Jacques Pepin)

Caesar Salad with Anchovies

Time Needed: 20-30 minutes - most can be done ahead
Ingredients
18 to 24 crisp, narrow leaves from the hearts of 2 heads of romaine lettuce, or a package of romaine hearts (about 1 pound)
1-2 cups plain toasted croutons
4 large cloves garlic, peeled
1 cup excellent olive oil
salt
2 anchovies
1 T. dijon mustard
4 large egg yolks
freshly ground black pepper
juice of 2 whole lemons
Worcestershire sauce
3 oz. freshly grated parmesan cheese, imported Parmigiano-Reggiano only

Directions:

To flavor the croutons, crush the garlic clove with the flat of a chef's knife, sprinkle on 1/4 teaspoon of salt, and mince well. Pour about a tablespoon of olive oil on the garlic and mash again with the knife, rubbing and pressing to make a soft purée.

Scrape the purée into the frying pan, add another tablespoon of oil, and warm over low-medium heat. Add the croutons and toss for a minute or two to infuse them with the garlic oil, then remove from the heat. Toast in the oven if desired. (For a milder garlic flavor, you can strain the purée though a small sieve into a pan before adding the extra croutons. Discard the bits of garlic.)

Rub the inside of a large wooden bowl with the garlic cloves. Remove the garlic cloves, and finely chop. Add the egg yolks, remaining garlic, anchovies and mustard to the bowl. Whisk until all of the ingredients are incorporated. Whisk in the lemon juice. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil, while whisking constantly. Whisk until all of the ingredients are incorporated. Season the dressing with salt and pepper. Gently tear the lettuce into bit-size portions and add to the salad bowl. Using a hand-held zester, grate the cheese into the bowl. Add the croutons. Toss the salad completely and reseason if necessary. Serve the salad on cold salad plates.

Cauliflower with Lemon Dressing

Time Needed: 20-30 minutes
Ingredients
1 head of cauliflower, cut into small florets
3 Tablespoons oil (hazelnut oil is great)
1 Tablespoon lemon juice
1 Tablespoon Dijon mustard
 1/3 cup cream
1/3 cup hazelnuts (or walnuts or pecans)
1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Directions:

Preheat oven to 450 F. In a sheet pan, toss the cauliflower with 2 Tablespoons of the oil and a little salt. Roast cauliflower until tender and lightly browned, stirring once or twice for even roasting, 15-20 minutes.

Meanwhile, toast the nuts in a dry skillet for a few minutes over medium-high heat, shaking pan or stirring to avoid burning. Nuts will continue to toast a little as they cool, so remove pan from heat before they finish toasting and set aside to cool.

In a large bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, mustard, cream, and remaining tablespoon of oil Add the roasted hot cauliflower, scraping any residual oil and brown cauliflower bits into the bowl. Add the nuts and freshly ground black pepper. Toss to coat. Serve warm. Serves 4.

Creamed Spinach

Time Needed: 10 minutes
Ingredients
2 pounds fresh spinach, washed and tough stems removed
2 tablespoons unsalted butter
1/2 cup finely chopped shallots
1 teaspoon minced garlic 		3/4 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
1/2 cup heavy cream

Directions: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil over high heat. Add the spinach and cook for 2 minutes. Drain in a fine mesh strainer, pressing with a large spoon to release as much water as possible. Finely chop and set aside.

Melt the butter in medium sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the shallots and garlic and cook, stirring, until soft and fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the spinach and cook, stirring, just until the liquid is released. Add the cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg, and cook until the cream is reduced by half, about 4 minutes. Remove from the heat and serve immediately.

Eggplant Lasagna

Ingredients
Olive Oil
1 lb. eggplant (2 small or 1 large)
Salt and pepper
1 egg, beaten
1/2 lb. whole milk ricotta cheese

1/2 c. grated Parmesan cheese		 1/2 large onion, chopped

2-3 Tablespoons Italian Herb Spices
1 teaspoon garlic powder

Eggplant Parmigiana

Time Needed: 65 minutes
Ingredients
Oil
2 large eggplants
All-purpose flour
4 eggs 		Bread crumbs
1 quart marinara sauce
8 ounces sliced dry mozzarella
4 ounces grated Romano cheese

Directions: Preheat oven to 350 degrees F and heat oil in a large pan, Dutch oven or deep-fryer. Peel the eggplant and slice lengthwise into 1/4-inch thick slices. Coat each side of the eggplant with the flour. In a separate bowl beat 4 eggs and dip the eggplant into the egg to coat both sides. Then take your bread crumbs and do the same to coat each side. Once the oil is hot, put the eggplant in the hot oil and fry until golden brown. You can also use a deep-fryer and leave in for about 2 to 3 minutes.

Once all of the eggplant has been fried, get a rectangular baking pan and start the layering by adding the marinara sauce to the bottom of the pan, then the eggplant, more sauce, fresh mozzarella, Romano cheese, and continue to layer until you have reached the top of the pan. Top off with sauce, mozzarella, and grated Romano.

Place the eggplant into the oven and cook for 20 to 25 minutes.

Ingredients
1/2 lb haricots verts or thin green beans, trimmed
2 Tablespoons olive oil
2 medium shallots, halved and thinly sliced (about 1/4 cup)
1 teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary (optional)
1 medium yellow bell pepper, cored and sliced 1/4 inch thick 		3 Tablespoons fresh orange juice
1 teaspoon orange jest
1/2 Tablespoon balsamic vinegar
3 cups lightly packed fresh baby spinach leaves
Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions: Bring water to boil in a pot with a steamer basket or steamer insert. Put the beans in the steamer and cover. Steam until bright green and just beginning to soften, about 2-3 minutes. Transfer the beans to a bowl and set aside.

Time Needed: 45 minutes
Ingredients
4 cups diced squash and zucchini (about 1 zucchini and 2 small yellow squash)
vegetable oil

3 Tablespoons butter
1/3 cup sour cream
 3/4 t. salt

2/3 cup grated Cheddar Cheese
2/3 cup crumbled crackers (Ritz)

Directions:  Turn oven on to 350 degrees. Cook the squash in a little vegetable oil over medium-low heat for 15-20 minutes until it has broken down.  Line a colander with a clean tea towel.  Put the cooked squash in the lined colander.  After it has cooled, squeeze the excess water from the squash using the tea towel.  Set aside.

Vegetable Tart

Time Needed: 3 hours
Ingredients
Crust
1 1/2 cups all purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon salt
6 tablespoons chilled unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
3 tablespoons chilled solid vegetable shortening, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
4 tablespoons (about) ice water
 Filling
5 ounces soft fresh goat cheese
1/2 cup whipping cream
1/4 cup sour cream
1/8 teaspoon salt
Pinch of cayenne pepper
3 large eggs 		Topping
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 small red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, cut into 1/2-inch strips
1/2 medium fennel bulb, cored, cut lengthwise into 1/2-inch strips
1 small zucchini (about 5 ounces), halved lengthwise, cut crosswise into 1/2-inch pieces
1 small yellow squash (about 5 ounces), halved lengthwise, cut crosswise into 1/2-inch pieces
1/2 cup fresh corn kernels
1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil

Directions:

For crust: Blend flour and salt in processor. Using on/off turns, cut in butter and shortening until pea-size pieces form. With machine running, add enough ice water by tablespoonfuls to form moist clumps. Gather dough into ball; flatten into disk. Wrap in plastic; refrigerate 1 hour. (Can be made 1 day ahead. Keep refrigerated. Soften dough slightly at room temperature before continuing.)

Roll out dough on lightly floured surface to 12-inch round. Transfer to 9-inch-diameter tart pan with removable bottom. Trim dough overhang to 1 inch. Fold overhang in; press to form double-thick sides. Pierce bottom of dough all over with fork. Refrigerate 1 hour.

Preheat oven to 400°F. Bake crust until golden, piercing with fork if bubbles form, about 20 minutes. Cool 5 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 375°F.

For filling and topping: Whisk cheese, cream, sour cream, salt, and cayenne in medium bowl to blend. Add eggs and whisk to blend. Pour into prepared crust. Bake until filling is set, about 20 minutes. Cool 10 minutes.

Meanwhile, heat oil in large skillet over medium heat. Add bell pepper and fennel and sauté until beginning to soften, about 4 minutes. Add zucchini, yellow squash, and corn; sauté until all vegetables are tender, about 12 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Mound vegetables on top of tart and sprinkle with basil. Serve warm or at room temperature.

White Asparagus with Airy Mayonnaise

Time Needed: 15 minutes
Ingredients
Asparagus and Lemon Vinaigrette
1 lemon
1/4 teaspoon minced shallot
1/8 teaspoon kosher salt
2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
freshly ground black pepper
1 16 ounce can of white Navarra asparagus, drained
leaves from 2 sprigs of fresh tarragon

 Airy Mayonnaise
1 egg + 1 egg yolk, room temperature
1 1/2 teaspoons sherry vinegar
1 teaspoon dijon mustard
1/4 cup olive oil + 1/4 cup canola oil
3/4 teaspoon kosher salt

Directions:

For vinaigrette: Zest 1/4 of the lemon into a small bowl, then squeeze in 1 1/2 teaspoons juice. Reserve the remaining lemon. Whisk in the shallot and salt. Slowly drizzle in the oil while whisking vigorously. Season to taste with pepper.

Arrange the asparagus on a serving platter or on individual plates and drizzle with the lemon vinaigrette. Zest the remaining lemon on top.

For air mayonnaise: Whip the egg and egg yolk with the whisk attachment of an electric mixon on mdium-high speed until super light, foamy and almost quadrupled in volume (about 5 minutes). Add the vinegar, mustard and salt and whip for a few seconds to combine. Reduce the speed to medium and slowing pour in the oil while whisking.

Spoon the airy mayonnaise onto the asparagus and top with tarragon leaves. Serve right away. Serves 2-3 people.
