Banana Nut Bread

Time Needed: 1 1/2 hours
Ingredients
1 cup (2 sticks) butter
2 cups sugar
2 teaspoons vanilla extract
3 eggs
2 cups mashed bananas
 3 cups flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
3/4 teaspoon salt
1 cup chopped pecans

Directions: Turn on oven to 350 degrees. Soften the butter a little. Beat together the sugar, butter, and vanilla until creamy. Add the eggs and mashed bananas and mix up. Add the flour, baking soda, salt,and nuts. Pour into a greased loaf pan. Bake for 1 hour, 10 minutes.

Chocolate Chip Oatmeal Cookies

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
3/4 cup flour
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 cup oatmeal
1/2 cup butter (1 stick)
1/2 cup sugar
 1/2 cup brown sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1 egg
1/2 cup chopped nuts (I use pecans)
1 - 6 ounce package of semi-sweet chocolate chips (or can use a little more if you want them full of chips

Directions: Turn on oven to 375 degrees. Mix together the flour, baking soda and salt. If you have a sifter, this is a good way to mix them up, but not necessary. Add the oatmeal and set aside. Soften the butter or margarine by either leaving it out at room temperature for a bit or by putting it in the microwave for about 20 seconds. In a large mixing bowl, beat together the butter, sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla until it is creamy. Then add the egg and mix a little more. Add the flour mixture and mix well. Then add the nuts and chocolate chips. If you are not using a non-stick cookie sheet, grease the sheet you are going to use with oil or margarine. Use a spoon to scoop up the cookie dough and put on cookie sheet in rounded spoonfuls. You can probably fit about 12 - 15 scoops on a sheet. Put in the oven and bake for about 10 - 12 minutes. Take up and let cool on a piece of waxed paper or aluminum foil. Fill cookie sheet again and put in oven. Keep going until you have used all the dough. Once they have cooled, you can stack them up in a cookie jar or on a plate.

Chocolate Frosting

Time Needed: 20 minutes
Ingredients
1/2 cup butter (1 stick)
3 squares unsweetened chocolate
1 lb confectioner's sugar (about 3 1/2 cups sifted)
 pinch of salt
4-5 Tablespoons hot water
1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Directions: Cream butter, then add sugar gradually. Melt chocolate in microwave and blend in. Stir in hot water a little at a time, adding just enough to give a good spreading consistency. Beat until fluffy. Add vanilla. Spread onto cooled cake. Makes enough frosting for tops and sides of 2 9-inch layers or 2 dozen cupcakes.

Chocolate Souffle

Time Needed: overnight + 45 minutes
Ingredients
1 cup whole milk
1 large egg
5 large egg yolks
1 Tablespoon sugar
1/3 cup flour 1 1/2 Tablespoons cornstarch		 1 Tablespoon cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes
1/2 Tablespoon sugar
1/8 teaspoon cream of tartar
1/2 cup cocoa
1/4 cup cognac
confectioners sugar for dusting 		2 ounces bittersweet chocolate chips
1/4 cup heavy cream
1/2 Tabplespoon unsalted butter

Directions: For the souffle base: In a medium saucepan, bring the milk to a boil over high heat, then remove from the heat and set aside.

In a large bowl, combine the egg, egg yolk, and sugar and whisk until blended. Combine the flour and cornstarch in a small bowl and stir with a whisk to blend. Add 2/3 of the flour mixture to the egg mixture and whisk until smooth. Add 1/4 cup of the hot milk to the egg mixture and mix well. Add another 1/4 cup of the milk and mix. Add the remaining flour mixture and whisk until smooth.

Bring the remaining milk back to a boil and add it to the base all at once, stirring constantly until smooth. Immediately stir in the butter until fully incorporated. The mixture should have the consistency of heavy cream. Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours or, preferably, overnight.

In a large bowl, beat the egg whites until frothy. Then beat in the sugar and cream of tartar and beat until soft peaks form. Add the cocoa powder and cognac.

Preheat the oven to 375. Butter and sugar some ramekins (4 1/2 ounce - 8 ounce size) and place in baking pan. Fold 1/4 of the egg whites into the souffle base and stir to combine. Then gently fold in the remaining egg whites. Divide the batter among the ramekins. Put hot water in the pan around the ramekins to reach about halfway up the sides of the dishes. Bake for 20 minutes or until puffed and golden brown.

Make the choclate sauce: Heat the cream and butter to a simmer. Remove from the heat and whisk in the chocolate chips. Whisk until smooth.

Transfer each ramekin to a serving plate, dust with confectioners sugar and serve with the chocolate sauce on the side.

Congo Squares

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
2 3/4 cup flour
2 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon salt
2/3 cup butter or margarine (1 stick + 3 Tablespoons)
 1 pound package of light brown sugar
3 eggs
1 cup chopped pecans
1 - 12 ounce package of semi-sweet chocolate chips

Directions: Turn on oven to 350 degrees. Mix together the flour, baking soda and salt. If you have a sifter, this is a good way to mix them up, but not necessary. Soften the butter or margarine by either leaving it out at room temperature for a bit or by putting it in the microwave for about 20 seconds. In a large mixing bowl, beat together the butter and brown sugar until it is creamy. Then add the eggs one at a time, beating after each one is added. Add the flour mixture and mix well. Then add the nuts and chocolate chips. Pour into a greased or non-stick pan (11 inch x 15 inch). Bake at 350 degrees for 20 to 30 minutes. It is done when you stick a toothpick in it and it does not come out all gooey. Take it out and let it cool. When almost cool, cut into squares.

Flourless Chocolate Tort

Time Needed: 1 hour to make, 2 hours to chill.
Ingredients
For Cake:
1 cup water
3/4 cup sugar
9 Tablespoons unsalted butter diced (1 stick + 1 Tablespoon)
18 ounces bittersweet or semisweet chocolate, chopped
6 large eggs		 For ganache:
1 cup heavy whipping cream
8 ounces bittersweet or semisweet chocolate, chopped
Lightly sweetened whipping cream or vanilla ice cream

Directions:

For cake:
  • Preheat oven to 350°F. Butter 10-inch-diameter springform pan. Line bottom of pan with parchment round; butter parchment. Wrap 3 layers of heavy-duty foil around outside of pan, bringing foil to top of rim. Combine 1 cup water and sugar in small saucepan. Bring to boil over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves. Simmer 5 minutes. Remove from heat.
  • Melt butter in large saucepan over low heat. Add chocolate and whisk until smooth. Whisk sugar syrup into chocolate; cool slightly. Add eggs to chocolate mixture and whisk until well blended. Pour batter into prepared pan. Place cake pan in large roasting pan. Add enough hot water to roasting pan to come halfway up sides of cake pan.
  • Bake cake until center no longer moves when pan is gently shaken, about 50 minutes. Remove from water bath; transfer to rack. Cool completely in pan.
For ganache:
  • Bring whipping cream to simmer in small saucepan over medium heat. Remove from heat. Add chocolate and whisk until smooth. Pour over top of cake still in pan. Gently shake pan to distribute ganache evenly over top of cake. Refrigerate cake in pan until ganache is set, about 2 hours. DO AHEAD: Can be made 2 days ahead. Cover and keep refrigerated.
  • Run knife around pan sides to loosen cake; release sides. Cut cake into wedges and serve with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Serves 16.

Aunt Dot's Ice Cream Pie

Time Needed: 15 minutes to make, plus freezer time of at least an hour
Ingredients
1 small package of semi-sweet chocolate chips
1/2 stick margarine (4 Tablespoons)
1 cup of Rice Krispies cereal
1/2 gallon of vanilla ice cream (Breyer's is best)

Directions: Take the ice cream out of the freezer to be getting a little soft. Put the chocolate chips into a bowl and put in the microwave for 1 minute. Add the margarine and put back in the microwave for another minute (or until melted slightly). Stir vigorously until the mixture is smooth. Add the Rice Krispies and stir together. Grease a pie pan with margarine. Put the chocolate mixture into the pie pan and shape into a crust as well as you can. Put the pie pan in the freezer for just a few minutes. This will harden the crust so it is easier to form. Finish forming into the shape of a pie crust and put it in the freezer for at least 10 more minutes. By now the ice cream should be soft enough to work with. So, take the crust out of the freezer and add ice cream to fill the pie pan to the top. Smooth out on top with the back of the spoon. Put it back in the freezer to harden. If you want, you can drizzle a little Hershey's syrup on the top just before you serve it.

Freda's Lemon Cake

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
1 package Lemon Cake Mix
1 package Lemon Jello
3/4 cup oil
3/4 cup water
4 eggs		 2 cups powdered sugar
juice of 2 lemons

Directions: Turn on oven to 325 degrees. Put lemon cake mix, lemon jello, and oil in a bowl and beat. Add water and beat. Add eggs, one at a time, mixing after each one. Put in a greased cake pan (large pan with hole in center works best). Put in the oven and cook about 50 minutes. You can check for doneness by sticking a toothpick in the cake. If it does not come out gooey, it is done. Take the cake out of the pan. Mix together the lemon juice and the powdered sugar. Pour over the cake while it is hot.

Lemon Tart

Time Needed: 4 hours
Ingredients
10 Tablespoons (1 1/4 sticks) unsalted butter
1/4 cup packed golden brown sugar
7 whole graham crackers, finely crushed

1 1/4 cups chilled whipping cream		 1/2 cup whole milk
1 teaspoon unflavored gelatin
1 cup plus 1 tablespoon sugar
1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
2 teaspoons grated lemon peel
2 large eggs

Directions: Melt 6 tablespoons butter in medium skillet over medium-high heat. Whisk in brown sugar. Whisk until mixture bubbles thickly, about 2 minutes. Mix in cracker crumbs; stir 1 minute. Using back of fork press warm crumb mixture over bottom and up sides of a 9-inch-diameter tart pan with removable bottom. Cool crust completely.

Pour milk into small custard cup. Sprinkle gelatin over. Let stand until gelatin softens, about 15 minutes. Whick 1 cup sugar, lemon juice, and lemon peel in heavy medium saucepan to blend well. Whisk in eggs, then remaining 4 tablespoons butter. Whisk over medium heat until custard thickens and just begins to boil, about 5 minutes; remove from heat. Add gelatin mixture; whisk until gelatin dissolves. Let stand until beginning to set, about 10 minutes. Pour warm filling into crust. Chill until filling is firm, at least 3 hours and up to 1 day.

Beat cream and remaining 1 tablespoon sugar in medium bowl until peaks form. Spoon cream over tart. Serve immediately or chill up to 4 hours. Makes 6 to 8 servings.

Maple Syrup

Time Needed: 10 minutes
Ingredients
2 cups sugar
1/2 cup brown sugar
1 cup water
1 teaspoon maple extract
1 cup light corn syrup (optional - to prevent sugar from crystallizing)

Directions: Place ingredients in sauce pan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes. Serve while still warm.

Strawberries w/ Balsamic Vinegar

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
2 pounds fresh strawberries
2 teaspoons sugar
2 teaspoons aged balsamic vinegar
handful of fresh mint leaves

Directions: Slice strawberries. Sprinkle with sugar and toss with balsamic vinegar. Cover and let sit for 30 - 90 minutes. Finely slice the mint leaves and add in just before serving.
