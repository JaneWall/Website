Ciabatta

Time Needed: 2- 3 hours
Ingredients
For biga
1 cup plus 1 tablespoon room-temperature water (75°F to 80°F)
1 1/4-ounce package dry yeast
3 1/3 cups bread flour
 For dough
3/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons room-temperature water (75°F to 80°F)
Pinch of dry yeast
1/2 cup plus 3 tablespoons semolina flour (or pasta flour)
 2 1/2 teaspoons salt
Additional semolina flour

Directions:

Make biga: Place water in processor. Sprinkle yeast over. Let stand until yeast dissolves, about 8 minutes. Add 1 cup flour; process until blended. Scrape down sides of work bowl. Add 1 cup flour; repeat processing and scraping. Add remaining 1 1/3 cups flour. Process until small moist clumps form. Gather dough into ball (dough will be firm); place in large bowl. Cover; chill overnight (biga will soften, resembling thick oatmeal in texture).

Make dough: Pull biga into walnut-size pieces; place in a clean large bowl. Add water, yeast and 1/2 cup plus 3 tablespoons semolina. Using 1 hand, squeeze ingredients together 2 minutes. Work dough 4 minutes by scooping sections from sides of bowl and pressing into center, blending into very soft, shaggy mass. Using spatula, scrape dough from sides of bowl into center. Let dough rest in bowl, uncovered, 10 minutes.

Sprinkle salt over dough. Using 1 hand, knead dough by rotating bowl 1/4 turn at a time, scooping dough from sides and folding down into center until dough starts to come away from sides of bowl, about 5 minutes. Scrape dough from hand and sides of bowl. Cover bowl with towel; let dough rest 20 minutes.

Rotating bowl 1/4 turn at a time, fold dough over onto itself 6 times; turn dough over in bowl. Cover with towel and let dough rest in bowl 20 minutes.

Bake bread: Preheat oven to 425°F. Sprinkle work surface with additional semolina. Turn dough out onto semolina. Using pastry scraper or large knife, cut dough in half; keep halves separated. Let stand, uncovered, 20 minutes.

Sprinkle 2 large baking sheets with additional semolina. Transfer each dough half, semolina side up, to 1 sheet. Stretch each dough half to 16x4-inch rectangle. Press fingertips into dough in several places to dimple surface (characteristic of this bread). Bake until golden brown, about 25 minutes. Cool. (Can be prepared 2 weeks ahead. Double-wrap in aluminum foil to freeze.)

Makes 2 loaves.

Fettuccine Alfredo

Time Needed: 20 - 30 minutes
Ingredients
1 pound of fettuccine pasta
1/2 teaspoon of salt
1 Tablespoon olive oil
 1 cup of cream
1 stick of butter
1 teaspoon of white pepper
1 wedge of parmesan cheese

Directions: Put water in large pot and cook at high heat. Grate the parmesan cheese and set it to the side. When the water starts to boil, put a skillet on the stove on medium heat. Add the olive oil and salt to the water. Put the butter and cream and white pepper into the skillet. Stir this as it melts. Put the fettuccine pasta into the boiling water. When the pasta is cooked (about 8 minutes), dump it into a colander. Stir the parmesan cheese into the butter and cream in the skillet. Then add the drained fettuccine and mix it up and serve.

Lasagna

Time Needed: 2 - 3 hours (can be made ahead and then heated)
Ingredients
1 pound of ground beef
1 pound hot Italian sausage links
1 Tablespoon olive oil
6 cloves garlic minced
1 onion finely chopped
1/2 bell pepper finely chopped
3 sprigs fresh thyme or 1 teaspoon dried thyme
1 stalk fresh basil or 1 teaspoon dried basil
3 stalks fresh oregano or 1 teaspoon dried oregano leaves
1 teaspoon salt 2 Tablespoons brown sugar 		3 large (29 oz.) cans of tomato sauce
10 - 12 lasagna noodles
1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
1/2 cup grated Romano cheese
24 ounces of Mozzarella cheese, grated
10 ounces of Provolone cheese, grated
2 pounds of Ricotta cheese
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon pepper
3 eggs, beaten

Directions: Heat a large skillet. Crumble the ground beef and cook over medium high heat until all the pink is gone. Take the meat out and pour out the grease. Cook the sausage links on the grill on low heat for about 30 minutes. Then slice the sausage into thin slices. Heat the skillet again and put in the olive oil. Then add the onions, garlic, and bell pepper. Cook about five or ten minutes (until translucent). Add the seasonings (the ones in the first column), tomato sauce, ground beef and sliced sausage. Simmer for one hour uncovered over low heat until thickened.

Cook lasagna noodles, then drain and rinse. Mix together the Ricotta cheese with 1 teaspoon of salt, the 1/2 teaspoon of pepper and the beaten eggs. Now take out a baking dish that is about 15" x 10" and at least 2 " deep. Put a small amount of the tomatoe sauce mixture in the bottom. Then put one layer of lasagna noodles, then 1/2 the Parmesan cheese, 1/2 the Romano cheese, 1/2 the Mozzarella cheese, 1/2 the Provolone cheese, all the Ricotta chesse mixture, 1/2 the tomato sauce, another layer of lasagna noodles, rest of the tomato sauce, rest of the Parmesan cheese, rest of the Romano cheese. You should have used all the ingredients now except for 1/2 of the Provolone and 1/2 the Mozzarella cheese. Cover and bake at 400 degrees for 45 minutes. The uncover it and add the rest of the Provolone and Mozzarella cheese on top. Bake uncovered for about 10 minutes. Remove and let stand about 15 minutes. Serves about 12 people.

Aunt Dot's Macaroni and Cheese

Time Needed: 1 1/2 hours
Ingredients
1 cup of macaroni elbow noodles
1/2 teaspoon of salt
2 cups of cheddar cheese grated
 3 cups of milk
2 eggs, beaten
margarine - about 3 Tablespoons

Directions: Put about 2 cups of water in a pot on the stove on high heat until the water is boiling. Then add the salt and macaroni to the pot. Let it cook for 10 minutes. The macaroni should be soft by now. Drain off the water. Grease a baking dish (9" by 13" dish) with margarine. Put about half the macaroni in the dish, then half the cheese. Then put the rest of the macaroni and then the rest of the cheese. Add the milk to the eggs you have beaten and then pour that mixture on top of the macaroni and cheese in the baking dish. Put 4 or 5 slices of margarine on the top. Put the macaroni and cheese in a cold oven (do not pre-heat the oven). Turn the oven on to bake at 325 degrees. Bake for 1 hour.

Pasta with Tomato and Basil

Time Needed: 15 minutes
Ingredients
1 lb. of pasta (I like Angel Hair, but use whatever you like.
3-4 cloves of minced garlic
1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
2 lbs. chopped tomatoes 		1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
salt
fresh ground black pepper

Directions: Put a large pot of water on stove with heat on high. Combine all the ingredients except for the pasta in a large bowl. When the water starts to boil, add some salt to the water (1 tablespoon approximately). Then add the pasta and cook to al dente.

Drain the pasta and add to the bowl containing all the other ingredients. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serves 6.

Pizza

Time Needed: 2 1/2 hours
Dough Ingredients
1 package of yeast (look in flour section of store)
1 cup warm water (not hot, just warm)
5 1/2 teaspoons of sugar
 3/4 teaspoon of salt
1/3 cup oil
2 3/4 cups flour
Other Pizza Ingredients
Spaghetti Sauce
cheese - mozzarella and / or provolone
meats - pepperoni, Italian sausage
veggies - olives, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms parmesan cheese 1 teaspoon oregano garlic - powder or crushed

Directions: Dissolve the yeast in the water in a medium to large bowl. Let sit for about 5 minutes. Add sugar, salt, and oil. Then stir in the flour until it holds together and doesn't stick to your hands. Cover the bowl and put in a warm place for the dough to rise for about an hour. Press the dough into a pan (12 inch round or rectangle). Let the dough rise for another hour. While it is rising, slice your meats and veggies that will go on top. Put half the cheese on top of the dough, then the meat and veggies. Spread sauce over the top. Cover with the other half of the cheese. Add parmesan cheese, oregano, and garlic to the top. Bake at 450 degrees for 20 - 30 minutes.

Wild Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto

Time Needed: 1 hour

6 cups chicken broth
1 cup dry white wine
2 tablespoons unsalted butter
1 cup finely chopped onion
Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
2 cups Arborio rice
5 ounces wild mushrooms, cooked and coarsely chopped, approximately 3/4 cup
7 ounces asparagus, cooked and cut into 1-inch pieces, approximately 1 1/2 cups
2 ounces grated Parmesan, approximately 1/2 cup
1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
1/2 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg

Directions: In an electric kettle or medium saucepan with a lid, combine chicken broth and white wine and heat just to simmering. Keep warm.

In a large 3 to 4-quart heavy saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the onions and a pinch of salt and sweat until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the rice and stir. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes or until the grains are translucent around the edges. Be careful not to allow the grains or the onions to brown.

Reduce the heat to low. Add enough of the wine and chicken stock just to cover the top of the rice. Stir or move the pan often, until the liquid is completely absorbed into rice. Once absorbed, add another amount of liquid just to cover the rice and continue stirring or moving as before. There should be just enough liquid left to repeat 1 more time. It should take approximately 35 to 40 minutes for all of the liquid to be absorbed. After the last addition of liquid has been mostly absorbed, add the mushrooms and asparagus and stir until risotto is creamy and asparagus is heated through. Remove from the heat and stir in the Parmesan, lemon zest, and nutmeg. Taste and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

*Cook's Note: If fresh wild mushrooms are not available, reconstituted dried mushrooms can be used instead. Place 2 ounces of dried mushrooms into a bowl and cover with warm water. Allow to sit for about 30 minutes or until all the mushrooms are soft and pliable.

Spaghetti a la Carbonara

Time Needed: 30 minutes
8 ounces of spaghetti
6 ounces (4 slices) of bacon
1/2 small onion, finely chopped
2 Tablespoons olive oil
pinch of salt and white pepper 		3/4 cup cream
3/4 cup grated parmesano reggiano cheese
3 eggs + 1 egg yolk
2 Tablespoons of butter

Directions: Put pot of water on to boil for pasta. While water is coming to a boil, chop bacon into small pieces and saute with chopped onion in a medium-sized frying pan. Remove bacon and onion onto paper towel and pour off grease. In a bowl, beat together the eggs and the egg yolk. When the water is boiling in the pasta pot, add some salt and the olive oil to the water and put in the spaghetti. Cook until al dente (the spaghetti package should tell you how long - usually about 8 minutes). While the spaghetti is cooking, put the butter, cream, and a little salt and white pepper in the frying fan over low heat and whisk together occassionally. When the spaghetti is done, drain it. Add the egg mixture and grated cheese to the butter and cream in the frying pan. Toss in the spaghetti immediately and serve. Serves 2-3 people as main dish or 3-5 as side dish.
