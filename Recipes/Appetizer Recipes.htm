7 Layer Dip

Time Needed: 15 minutes
Ingredients
1 can refried bean dip
1 - 2 cups guacamole w/ jalapeno peppers
1 - 8 or 12 ounce sour cream
1 package taco seasoning mix
 1 bunch scallions, chopped
2 tomatoes, diced
1 can black olives, chopped
1 - 2 cups grated cheddar cheese
1 bag tortilla chips

Directions: Get out a large plate or platter. Spread the bean dip on the plate. Then put a layer of guacamole. Mix the taco seasoning mix into the sour cream and then put on top of the guacamole. Add scallions, then tomatoes, then olive, and top with cheddar cheese. Serve with tortilla chips.

Country Bread with Spinach and Reduced Beef Jus (Ducasse)

Time Needed: 15 minutes
Ingredients
1 Tablespoon unsalted butter
10 ounces fresh spinach, stems trimmed and carefully washed
1 large garlic clove, peeled
1/2 cup heavy cream 		1/4 cup pan juices from roast beef
fine sea salt
freshly ground black pepper
Eight 1/2-inch thick slices French baguette

Directions: Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat and cook so that the butter foams up and subsides twice. At this point it should be a light, fragrant, nut-brown. Add the spinach. Stick a dinner fork through the garlic clove so that the tines protrude about 1/2 inch and stir the spinach in the butter with the fork. Stirring gently, pour in the cream little by little. Simmer until the cream has reduced by half, then add the pan drippings little by little. Add salt and pepper to taste. Reduce the heat to low and simmer while you toastthe bread.

Preheat the broiler. Toast the bread slices to deep golden brown and place on a baking sheet. Divide the spinach among the toasts. Set under the broiler for about 30 seconds, until the sauce begins to bubble and turn light brown. Serve immediately, two toasts per serving. Can be served either as an appetizer or a side dish. Serves 4.

Crab and Avocado Stack with Shrimp and Tomato Coulis

Time Needed: 30 minutes (but can make it ahead)
Ingredients
Guacamole :
2 ripe avocados
1 Tablespoon pico de gallo (from Whole Foods fresh section)
3 - 4 sprigs fresh cilantro, chopped
1 Tablespoon fresh lime juice
salt to taste 		Tomato Coulis
1/2 cup Pace picante medium sauce
2 Tablespoons Chardonnay
1 teaspoon honey
1/8 teaspoon cajun spices 		Crab Á shrimp
1 jar of premium lump crab (SamsClub)
1 Tablespoon mayonnaise (approx.)
1/8 teaspoon cajun spices
squeeze fresh lemon juice
16 fresh cooked and chilled shrimp

Directions: Pulse the picante sauce in a blender until smooth. Add the Chardonnay, honey and spices to blended tomato mixture in a saucepan. Simmer over low to medium heat, then cool and refrigerate until chilled.

Cook and chill shrimp.

Mash up the avocado in a bowl. Add in some lime juice, a little salt, the pico de gallo and cilantro. Taste and adjust as needed for your guacamole.

Mix the crab meat with enough mayonnaise for it to hold together. Mix in the cajun spices, then squeeze a little lemon on it.

Assemble Line 7 - 8 four ounce ramekins with plastic wrap. The plastic wrap pieces should be long enough to fold back over the top when they are filled. Divide the guacamole between the ramekins. Add the crab mixture on top of each. Fold the plastic wrap over the top of each ramekin and refrigerate. When ready to serve, turn each ramekin upside down on a plate and remove the ramekin and plastic wrap. Garnish the plate with the tomato coulis and top each stack with 2 shrimp. Serves 7-8.

Gouzères

Time Needed: 30-45 minutes
Ingredients
1 cup milk
1/4 lb (1 stick) butter
1 teaspoon kosher salt
1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
pinch of nutmeg 		1 cup all-purpose flour
4 extra-large eggs
1/2 cup grated Gruyère cheese, plus extra for sprinkling
1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
1 egg beaten with 1 teaspoon water, for egg wash

Directions: Preheat the oven to 425 degrees. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.

In a saucepan, heat the milk, butter, salt, pepper, and nutmeg over medium heat, until scalded (just below boiling point). Add the four all at once and beat it vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture comes together. Cook, stirring constantly, over low heat for 2 minutes. The flour will begin to coat the bottom of the pan. Dump the hot mixture into the bowl of a food processor fitted with the steel blade. Immediately add the eggs, Gruyère, and Parmesan and pulse until the eggs are incorporated and the dough is smooth and thick.

Use 2 spoons to scoop out the mixture and shape the puffs with damp fingers into little balls. Place on lined baking sheets. Brush the top of each puff lightly with the egg wash and sprinkle with a pinch of Gruyère. Bake for 15 minutes, or until golden brown outside but still soft inside. Makes about 40 puffs.

Mushroom Rolls

Time Needed: 1 1/2 hours
Ingredients
1 pound mushrooms, finely chopped
1/2 cup (1 stick) butter
2 Tablespoons flour
1 1/2 teaspoons salt
2 cups light cream
 2 teaspoons lemon juice
1 teaspoon onion salt
1 1/2 loaves sliced white sandwich bread
4 Tablespoons melted butter

Directions: Put the chopped mushrooms and the stick of butter in a pan and cook for 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. A dd the flour and mix well, then add the salt. Stir in the cream and cook, stirring constantly, until thick. Add the lemon juice and onion salt and cool. Remove the crusts from the bread and roll slices very thin with a rolling pin. Spread some mushroom mixture on each slice of bread and roll up. Place on a baking sheet, seam side down. Place in the freezer for at least 10 minutes. At this point, you can put them into freezer containers for use at any future time. When ready to serve, pre-heat oven to 400 degrees. Cut the mushroom rolls into thirds and put the mushroom rolls on cookie sheet, seam side down. Brush the rolls with melted butter and bake for 15-20 minutes. Makes 7-8 dozen.

Pastry Wrapped Brie with Bacon & Date Chutney

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
4 slices thick-cut bacon, cooked until crisp and crumbled (1/4 cup)
2 Tbsp. mango chutney, chopped
1/4 cup chopped walmuts
1/2 cup chopped Medjool dates (about 6 dates)
1/2 Tbsp. chopped fresh rosemary
 One sheet ready rolled refrigerator pie crust (1/2 15 oz pkg)
1 Ile de France Brie cheese (7.7 oz)
1 large egg, beaten, for glaze
rosemary sprigs, for garnish

Directions: In a medium bowl, stir together dates, bacon, chutney, walnuts and rosemary. Finely chop or grind remaining 1/4 cup walnuts and place in a small dish. Place pastry on a board. Mound date mixture in center and spread level to a 4" circle. Place Brie on top. Brush exposed pastry with water. Gather and pleat pastry to cover Brie. Snip off excess dough and use to fill in any holes. Press with hands to flatten and seal seams. Invert onto parchment lined baking sheet. If desired, wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate.

Preheat oven to 400. Bake for 20 minutes; brush with egg and bake 5 minutes more or until golden brown. Set aside to cool slightly, about 30 minutes. Transfer to a platter and garnish with rosemary sprigs. Use a serrated kife to cut into wedges.

Puff Pastry with Mushrooms, Ham, and Gruyere (Ducasse)

Time Needed: 20 minutes
Ingredients
One 9" by 9" sheet of puff pastry, thawed
1 large egg yolk, beaten, for glaze
2 Tablespoons unsalted butter
1/2 pound white mushrooms, cut into small dice 		1/2 cup finely chopped ham
1/2 cup finely shredded Gruyere cheese
2 Tablespoons heavy cream
Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Using a sharp knife, cut the pastry dough into small circles or squares (1" diameter or 1" square). Place the dough pieces on a baking sheet and brush the tops lightly with the beaten egg. Bake in the center of the oven until the dough puffs up and turns golden brown, 12 to 14 minutes. Gently cut each pastry crosswise through through the center and set aside on the baking sheet. Leave the oven on.

Melt the butter in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring frequently, until they soften and begin to give up their moisture but do not brown, 3 to 4 minutes. Stir in the ham and cheese and cook until the cheese softens and begins to melt, 1 to 2 minutes. Add the cream and stir well, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from the heat.

Spoon about 1 1/2 teaspoons of the mushroom mixture onto the bottom half of each pastry puff and cover with the top half. Return the filled pastries to the oven for 3 to 4 minutes to heat through. Serve immediately. Makes about 36 hors d'oeuvres.

Puff Pastry with Spinach and Comte Cheese (Ducasse)

Time Needed: 20 minutes
Ingredients
One 9" by 9" sheet of puff pastry, thawed
1 large egg yolk, beaten, for glaze
2 Tablespoons unsalted butter
1/2 pound baby spinach, tough stems removed 		1/2 cup finely shredded Comte, aged Fontina or Gruyere cheese
2 Tablespoons heavy cream
Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Using a sharp knife, cut the pastry dough into small circles or squares (1" diameter or 1" square). Place the dough pieces on a baking sheet and brush the tops lightly with the beaten egg. Bake in the center of the oven until the dough puffs up and turns golden brown, 12 to 14 minutes. Gently cut each pastry crosswise through through the center and set aside on the baking sheet. Leave the oven on.

Melt the butter in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the spinach and cook, stirring frequently, until soft and wilted, about 3 minutes. Stir in the cream and cheese and cook until the cheese softens and begins to melt, 1 to 2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from the heat.

Spoon about 1 1/2 teaspoons of the spinach mixture onto the bottom half of each pastry puff and cover with the top half. Return the filled pastries to the oven for 3 to 4 minutes to heat through. Serve immediately. Makes about 36 hors d'oeuvres.

Salmon Mousse

Time Needed: 2 hours to overnight
Ingredients
3 ounces smoked salmon
1/2 shallot, minced
1/2 Tablespoon horseradish (if using fresh, also add a dash of vinegar)
1/4 cup creme fraiche
1/8 teaspoon ground white pepper
 pinch salt
1/4 cup heavy whipping cream
dill sprigs for garnish
salmon roe for garnish

Directions: Combine salmon, shallot, horseradish, creme fraiche, white pepper and salt in food processor and puree until smooth. Cover and refrigerate at least one hour or up to 12 hours.

Whip the cream in a deep bowl, then fold it into the puree. Serve the mousse on small tasting spoons and top each serving with a dill sprig and a little salmon roe. Serves 20.

Spinach Gruyere Puff Pastry Rolls

Time Needed: 30 minutes
Ingredients
1 (10-ounce) package frozen chopped spinach, thawed
4 tablespoons butter, divided
1 cup sliced fresh mushrooms
 4 ounces Gruyere cheese, grated
1 (17 1/2-ounce) package frozen puff pastry sheets, thawed

Directions: Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.

Drain spinach well, pressing between layers of paper towels to remove excess moisture. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook, for 5 minutes. Stir together spinach, mushrooms and cheese; set aside. Roll 1 pastry sheet into a 13 by 11-inch rectangle. Melt remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Brush 1/2 melted butter over pastry. Spread 1/2 spinach mixture over butter. Roll-up, jellyroll fashion, starting with the long side. Repeat procedure with remaining pastry sheet, butter, and spinach mixture. (Wrap rolls in plastic wrap and refrigerate up to 2 days, if desired.) Cut rolls into 1/4-inch thick slices. Place on lightly greased baking sheets. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until golden brown. Makes 12-15 servings.

Spring Rolls

Time Needed: 1 hour
Ingredients
1 lb shrimp with shells on
1/2 cup water
2 Tbsp white vinegar
1 Tbsp. rice wine
1 tsp. salt
 2 Tbsp. canola oil
8 ounces pork loin
package of rice vermicelli or rice sticks
1 (12-ounce) package dried rice paper
1 head butter lettuce
1 cup bean sprouts
 1/2 bunch fresh cilantro
1/4 cup fresh mint
1/4 cup fresh Thai basil
1/3 cup Hoisin sauce
1/3 cup peanut sauce

Directions: Step 1: Get ingredients ready. In a frying pan, combine 1/2 cup water, vinegar, rice wine and salt. Bring to a boil. Add shrimp, simmer until just pink. Remove shrimp (save the water), cool, peel and cut in half lengthwise. Set aside on top of a bowl of ice. Heat oil in a pan and sear over medium high heat until brown on both sides. Then add the liquid from cooking shrimp to the pan, simmer pork for 15 minutes or until tender. Remove from heat, drain and cut into thin slices. Set aside. Heat a pot of water to a boil and add about 1 large handful of uncooked rice vermicelli. Cook until al dente. Drain and add cold water to the vermicelli so it won't stick together. In a small bowl, mix the Hoisin and peanut sauces (you will dip the finished rolls in these at the table). Now your ingredients are ready!

Put warm water in a bowl or pyres dish about 1 inch deep. Place parchment paper or waxed paper on the counter. Dip a couple of rice papers in the warm water and then lay them out on the parchment paper. At one edge of the rice paper, place a piece of lettuce. Then put on a few bean sprouts, then some vermicelli. Add a slice of pork and 2 shrimp halves. Top with herb leaves. Then roll like a burrito. Place completed rolls on a serving tray (perhaps lined with wax paper). Serve with the dipping sauce and some soy sauce. Makes 20 - 30 rolls.

Stuffed Portabella Mushrooms

Time Needed: 20 minutes
Ingredients
2 large portabella mushrooms
1/2 small onion, chopped
Italian sausage (optional)
 2 T. extra virgin olive oil
1/4 c. bread crumbs
1/4 c. grated parmesan cheese

Directions: Remove and finely chop mushroom stems. Brown crumbled Italian sausage. Remove drained sausage to a bowl. Saute mushroom stems and finely chopped onion until softened. Put in bowl with sausage. Add bread crumbs and parmesan cheese. Put mushrooms, gill sides down, on a baking sheet and brush tops with 1/2 T. olive oil. Broil mushrooms 2 to 3 inches from heat for 3 minutes. Turn mushrooms over and season with salt and pepper. Broil another minute or two. Add stuffing to mushrooms and return to broil until the stuffing is golden brown (1-2 minutes). Serves 2-4 people.
